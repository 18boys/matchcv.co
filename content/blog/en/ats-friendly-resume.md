An ATS-friendly resume is a resume that applicant tracking systems can parse correctly and recruiters can scan quickly.

It is not a resume that tricks software. It is a resume that uses clear structure, relevant keywords, and honest evidence so your experience is easier to match to the job.

## ATS-friendly resume example structure

Use this order for most roles:

- Name and contact details
- Target title or short summary
- Skills
- Experience
- Projects or certifications
- Education

This structure is predictable, which makes it easier for software to classify your content and easier for recruiters to scan.

## Example summary

Weak:

- Hardworking professional looking for a challenging role where I can grow and contribute.

ATS-friendly:

- Data Analyst with 4 years of experience in SQL, Tableau, revenue reporting, and churn analysis for B2B SaaS teams.

The second version includes a target role, years of experience, hard skills, and domain context.

## Example skills section

Weak:

- Communication, teamwork, leadership, Microsoft Office, detail-oriented

ATS-friendly:

- Analytics: SQL, Tableau, Excel, cohort analysis, churn reporting
- Marketing: lifecycle campaigns, segmentation, A/B testing, HubSpot
- Operations: forecasting, pipeline reporting, stakeholder management

Grouped skills are easier to scan and more keyword-rich without feeling stuffed.

## Example experience bullet

Weak:

- Responsible for customer reporting.

ATS-friendly:

- Built Tableau customer health dashboards for 12 account managers, helping reduce at-risk account review time by 35%.

The stronger bullet includes a tool, audience, metric, and business outcome.

## What makes a resume ATS-friendly

The main ingredients are:

- Standard headings
- Selectable text
- Simple layout
- Relevant job-specific keywords
- Clear job titles and dates
- Bullets with measurable outcomes
- File format that preserves text order

No single item guarantees interviews. Together, they reduce the chance that your resume is filtered or misunderstood.

## What makes a resume ATS-unfriendly

Avoid:

- Two-column layouts with important content split across the page
- Tables for work experience
- Images containing text
- Icons instead of written labels
- Contact details in headers or footers
- Unusual section headings
- Keyword lists with no proof
- Tiny fonts and crowded spacing

These choices can hurt parsing or make recruiters work too hard.

## ATS-friendly does not mean boring

Your resume can still look professional. The key is to keep the structure simple and let the content carry the value.

Good design choices:

- Clear spacing
- Consistent heading sizes
- Simple dividers
- One readable accent color
- Strong bullet hierarchy
- No decorative elements that contain important text

## ATS-friendly resume checklist

Before you submit, verify:

- The resume has one clear target role
- Contact details are normal text
- Skills match the target job description
- Important keywords appear naturally
- Bullets include outcomes or metrics
- Dates and company names are easy to identify
- The file text can be copied and pasted in order
- The layout works in one column
