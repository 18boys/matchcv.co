If your resume gets no responses, the problem may not be your entire career history. Often, the resume is either hard to parse, too generic for the job, or missing the exact signals recruiters search for.

ATS rejection is rarely about one magic score. It is usually a combination of formatting, keyword match, role fit, and recruiter filters.

Start by checking whether your resume has obvious issues with the [free ATS resume checker](https://matchcv.co/free-ats-checker). Then review the common problems below.

## Reason 1: Your resume does not match the job description

The most common problem is a generic resume. If you use the same version for every job, it may not contain the specific keywords, tools, or outcomes in the posting.

Fix:

- Pull required skills from the job description
- Add only the skills you genuinely have
- Rewrite 2 to 4 bullets to reflect the target role
- Use the employer's wording when it is accurate

If the role asks for "revenue forecasting," do not rely on a vague phrase like "business planning" if you have forecasting experience.

## Reason 2: Important keywords are missing

Recruiters often search by hard skills, tools, job titles, certifications, and domain terms.

Missing examples:

- The job requires Salesforce, but your resume only says CRM
- The job requires SQL, but your resume only says data analysis
- The job requires lifecycle marketing, but your resume only says email campaigns

Fix these by adding truthful keywords to your skills and bullets.

## Reason 3: Your format is difficult to parse

ATS systems can struggle with design-heavy resumes.

Risky format choices:

- Tables
- Text boxes
- Two-column layouts
- Headers and footers
- Icons without text labels
- Image-only PDFs

Use a clean [ATS-friendly resume template](https://matchcv.co/templates) if you want to remove formatting risk quickly.

## Reason 4: Your section headings are unclear

Recruiters and software expect common headings. If your experience section is called "Impact Timeline," the parser may not classify it correctly.

Use standard headings:

- Summary
- Skills
- Experience
- Education
- Certifications
- Projects

This is one place where creativity hurts more than it helps.

## Reason 5: Your bullets describe tasks, not results

A resume can contain the right keywords and still look weak if it does not prove impact.

Weak:

- Helped manage social media campaigns

Stronger:

- Managed LinkedIn and email campaigns for 4 product launches, generating 1,800 qualified leads and increasing demo bookings by 22%

The stronger bullet gives a recruiter a reason to keep reading.

## Reason 6: Your resume is too broad

Many candidates try to look flexible by writing a resume for every possible role. That usually makes the resume less compelling for any one role.

Fix:

- Choose one target role per resume version
- Align the summary with that role
- Prioritize the most relevant experience
- Move unrelated details lower or remove them

A focused resume is easier to rank, scan, and remember.

## Reason 7: The file itself is broken

Some resumes look fine visually but fail the plain text test. Copy your final resume text and paste it into a plain text editor.

If you see broken order, missing sections, or unreadable characters, rebuild the file with a simpler layout.

## How to fix ATS rejection risk

Use this workflow:

1. Run a basic format check.
2. Extract keywords from the job description.
3. Compare those terms with your resume.
4. Rewrite the most relevant bullets.
5. Save as a clean DOCX or text-based PDF.
6. Apply with the tailored version.
