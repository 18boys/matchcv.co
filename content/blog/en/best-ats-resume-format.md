The best ATS resume format is not the most creative format. It is the format that keeps your information in the right order after software extracts the text.

That usually means a clean, single-column resume with standard headings, selectable text, simple bullets, and no layout tricks that hide important details.

## The best format for most ATS systems

Use this structure:

- Name and contact information
- Target title or short professional summary
- Skills grouped by category
- Work experience in reverse chronological order
- Projects, certifications, or education
- Optional extras only if they support the target role

This format works because it gives both the parser and the recruiter a predictable reading path.

## Use standard section headings

ATS parsers are trained around common resume sections. Use headings like:

- Summary
- Skills
- Experience
- Projects
- Education
- Certifications

Avoid creative labels such as "My Journey," "Core Superpowers," or "Where I Made an Impact." They may look memorable, but they make your resume harder to classify.

## Avoid tables, columns, and text boxes

Tables and columns can scramble reading order. A two-column resume might show your skills on the left and experience on the right, but a parser may read those sections in the wrong sequence.

Avoid:

- Two-column layouts for important content
- Tables for skills or experience
- Text boxes
- Icons instead of written labels
- Headers and footers for contact details
- Graphics with embedded text

If design matters to you, choose a clean [ATS-safe resume template](https://matchcv.co/templates) that balances readability and polish.

## Keep contact details in plain text

Your name, email, phone, LinkedIn URL, and portfolio should be normal selectable text near the top of the document.

Do not rely on:

- Icons without labels
- QR codes
- Image-based contact blocks
- Header or footer fields

Recruiters need this information, and parsing software needs to extract it reliably.

## Use simple bullet points

Use standard bullet characters and keep each bullet focused on one achievement.

Good bullet format:

- Led migration from manual reporting to Tableau dashboards, reducing weekly reporting time by 8 hours and improving forecast visibility for 14 sales managers

This bullet is easy to read, includes relevant keywords, and gives measurable evidence.

Avoid bullets that are too vague:

- Responsible for reports
- Helped with dashboards
- Worked with team members

## Use readable fonts and spacing

ATS software mostly cares about text extraction, but recruiters still read the resume. Use common fonts and enough spacing to scan quickly.

Good choices:

- Arial
- Calibri
- Helvetica
- Times New Roman
- Georgia

Keep the document clean. A resume that is technically parseable but visually crowded still hurts your chances with humans.

## PDF vs DOCX

If the employer gives instructions, follow them. If not, use a clean DOCX or a text-based PDF exported from a word processor.

Avoid image-only PDFs and files exported from design tools that turn text into shapes. A quick test is to open the final file, select all text, copy it, and paste it into a plain text editor. If the order is broken, your ATS parsing may be broken too.

## ATS resume format checklist

Before applying, check:

- Single-column layout
- Standard section headings
- Selectable text
- No important content in headers or footers
- No tables for core resume content
- No text hidden in images
- Clear job titles and dates
- Skills grouped in plain text
- Bullets with action, context, and results
