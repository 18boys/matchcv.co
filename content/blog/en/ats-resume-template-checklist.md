An ATS resume template should make your experience easier to read, not just make the page look designed. The wrong template can hide your skills, scramble your work history, or make contact details hard to parse.

Use this checklist before choosing a template. If you want a safer starting point, browse MatchCV's [ATS-friendly resume templates](https://matchcv.co/templates).

## Layout checklist

Choose a template with:

- One main content column
- Clear top-to-bottom reading order
- Contact details in the document body
- Standard sections
- Enough spacing between roles
- No important text inside graphics

Be careful with:

- Sidebars
- Two-column designs
- Skill bars
- Icons without labels
- Text boxes
- Tables

Some two-column templates can look clean, but they add parsing risk. Use them only if the text still copies in the correct order.

## Font checklist

Use readable, common fonts:

- Arial
- Calibri
- Helvetica
- Georgia
- Times New Roman

Avoid fonts that are decorative, compressed, or difficult to read at small sizes. A recruiter may spend only seconds scanning the resume, so clarity matters.

## Section checklist

Your template should support these sections:

- Summary
- Skills
- Experience
- Education
- Certifications
- Projects

Not every resume needs every section, but the template should not force unusual labels or hide standard headings.

## Skills section checklist

A good skills section is grouped and specific.

Example:

- Analytics: SQL, Tableau, cohort analysis, churn reporting
- Marketing: lifecycle campaigns, segmentation, A/B testing
- Tools: HubSpot, Salesforce, Google Analytics, Figma

Avoid long ungrouped keyword lists. They are harder to scan and look less credible.

## Experience section checklist

The experience section should make each role easy to parse:

- Company name
- Job title
- Location if relevant
- Dates
- 3 to 6 bullets
- Measurable outcomes

Avoid templates where dates float far away from role titles or where company names are embedded in graphics.

## File type checklist

A good template should export cleanly to:

- DOCX
- Text-based PDF

After exporting, use the plain text test. Copy the final resume and paste it into a plain text editor. If the text order is broken, simplify the layout or choose another template.

## Visual polish checklist

ATS-safe does not mean ugly. Good templates use:

- Clear hierarchy
- Consistent spacing
- Simple dividers
- Readable font sizes
- Minimal color
- No decorative elements that carry important information

The best design makes the content easier to read.

## Template red flags

Avoid templates with:

- Large headshots
- Rating bars for skills
- Dense sidebars
- Heavy icons
- Multiple columns of core content
- Text layered on background shapes
- Nonstandard section names

These choices can distract recruiters and create parsing problems.

## Choose the template, then tailor the content

A template solves structure. It does not solve job fit. After choosing a clean template, compare the resume content to the job description.
